What is a Will?

A will, also known as a testament, is a legal document that outlines your wishes—whether it’s who should care for your children or how your assets should be distributed among your loved ones and dependents. Without a will, the state steps in to make those decisions, which can lead to financial hardship or even disputes among your family members.

If you haven’t created a will yet, or if you need to update an existing one, we’re here to help. With FreeWill, you can draft your will for free—because where there’s a Will, there’s a better way forward.

Why Do I Need a Will?

A will ensures that your wishes are clearly understood and followed when you're no longer around—and that the people you care about are protected. Having a will in place helps you:

  • Provide better support for your spouse and children
  • Make sure your property, money, and assets are distributed according to your wishes
  • Avoid unnecessary delays in settling your estate
  • Save your estate thousands of rands in legal and administrative costs
  • Offer peace of mind to you and your loved ones

A will isn’t just a document—it’s a vital part of securing your family’s future.

Who Should Have a Will?

Everyone should have a will—regardless of age or stage of life. It's especially important if you:

  • Are married or divorced
  • Have children or other dependants
  • Are a young professional starting to build wealth
  • Are elderly or facing health challenges
  • Own property or significant assets
  • Possess valuable or sentimental items
  • Have a trust, offshore assets, or investments
  • Support others financially

No matter your situation, having a will ensures your wishes are respected and your loved ones are protected.
